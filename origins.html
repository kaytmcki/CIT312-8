Cultivation

Premium matcha is made from shade-grown tea leaves called tencha. The plants are sheltered from direct sunlight for 20–30 days before harvest, which boosts chlorophyll and amino acids—giving matcha its vibrant, bright green color and rich, umami flavor.

History & Significance

Originating in ancient China and perfected in Japan, matcha has a rich history deeply intertwined with spiritual and cultural practices. The ceremonial preparation of matcha using traditional tools like the chawan (tea bowl) and chasen (bamboo whisk) symbolizes mindfulness, respect, and harmony. Over centuries, matcha evolved from a meditative ritual into a celebrated beverage prized for its vibrant color, unique flavor, and numerous health benefits.

Benefits

Matcha is packed with antioxidants, boosts metabolism, and enhances mood and concentration. It provides a calm energy boost without the jitters of coffee. These health benefits make it a favorite for wellness enthusiasts around the globe.
